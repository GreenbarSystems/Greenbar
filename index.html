What is a CSV file?
CSV stands for Comma-Separated Values. It’s a plain-text spreadsheet file where each line is one transaction and the columns — date, description, amount — are separated by commas. Files end in .csv.
What to pick at your bank
When your bank lists export options, choose CSV — sometimes labeled “Comma-Separated Values,” “Excel-compatible CSV,” or just “spreadsheet.”
Other formats Greenbar accepts
If CSV isn’t offered, .tsv (tab-separated) and .txt files with the same row-per-transaction layout work too.
Formats that won’t work
PDFs (statements), OFX / QFX (Quicken), and Excel workbooks (.xls, .xlsx) aren’t plain text and can’t be read. Pick CSV instead.

What each screen does
Summary
Your home screen. Shows your monthly net, income vs expenses, and top spending categories. Switch months with the buttons at the top, or tap All for a combined view.
Budget
A side-by-side look at your budget targets and what you actually spent. Green means under budget, red means over. Categories you haven't budgeted for are listed separately at the bottom.

Full searchable transaction list grouped by date. Use the search bar to filter by merchant name or category. Shows income and expenses with color coding.
Settings
Set everything up: your bank's column names, words that mark income, transactions to leave out, rules for re-sorting merchants into categories, and your monthly budget targets. Changes take effect on your next import.

Under the Hood
How it’s built
Greenbar installs from the App Store or Google Play and then runs entirely on your phone. Importing, categorizing and saving all happen on-device — no internet connection is needed for day-to-day tracking.
→ A normal app — install it from your app store; no separate Greenbar account to create
→ Your data is saved in Greenbar’s private storage on your device. Only Greenbar can read it.
→ Works offline. No connection is needed to track, categorize or save your spending.
→ Nothing loaded from the internet — no outside files and no trackers of any kind
What happens when you import a file
Everything happens instantly on your device. Your file is never sent anywhere.
1
Read the file
Greenbar reads your Bank Transactions and works out which column is which — date, description, amount. If your bank uses unusual column names, you can set them in Settings.
2
Sort into categories
Each transaction gets a category. Banks label things differently, so Greenbar translates common names — “Restaurants/Dining” becomes “Dining Out,” for example. You can customize these rules in Settings.
3
Build your dashboard
Transactions are grouped by month, totals are calculated, and the Summary screen updates immediately.
4
Handle overlapping months
If you import a file that covers months you’ve already imported, Greenbar asks what you want to do — keep the old data, use the new data, or combine both.
5
Save everything
Your transactions and settings are saved on your device. They’ll be there next time you open Greenbar — no sign-in needed.

How your Health Score is calculated
Each month gets a score from 0 to 100, which becomes a letter grade. It’s made up of three things:
Did you save money? 40 pts
Saving 20% or more of your income earns full marks. Spending more than you earned scores zero.
Did you stick to your budget? 40 pts
Each budget category is scored on how close your actual spending was to your target. Being within 10% is perfect. If you haven’t set a budget yet, this part is neutral.
Are you tracking everything? 20 pts
Having 5 or more spending categories with real data earns full marks. This rewards importing complete statements rather than just a few transactions.
Grades: A (90+)  B (80+)  C (70+)  D (60+)  F (below 60)

Bank Transactions tips
Greenbar works with Bank Transactions exported from any bank. It automatically looks for columns named things like “Date,” “Description,” “Amount,” and “Category.” If your bank uses different names, you can set them in Settings.
→ Date column: Works with common date formats like 05/04/26, 05/04/2026, or 2026-05-04.
→ Amount column: Negative numbers are expenses, positive are income. Dollar signs and commas are ignored.
→ Category column: Optional. Greenbar will sort uncategorized transactions using the Description instead.
Files from most US banks import without any setup. If something looks wrong, check Settings → Column Mapping.
